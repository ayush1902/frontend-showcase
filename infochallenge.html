Design, Ideation and Workflow in Information Challenge

Challenge

This was the first information challenge at School of Information University of Michigan, where we had to work with interdisciplinary teams and come up with a idea to pitch. It was true learning experience in ideation, team building , brainstorming and pitching.

Approach and my role

eam was epitome of diversity. An architect, a history major, a graphic designer, a Product developer and finally a Learning experience designer (me). Initially, we had to brainstorm. There were few things that I learnt from the brainstorming session -

After brainstorming, our next step was ideation. Ideas came from across diverse range of passions and backgrounds. We finally resorted to voting and narrowed it down until we reached a common ground.

My Role

As I am primarily interested in learning more about people, the idea of knowing about passions of the team came in handy. Also, I insisted on solidifying the problem as compared to jumping directly on wireframes and sketches. I also increased team communication by white boarding, co-working and generating ideas for other discussion.I was also involved in competitor analysis and market research. One interesting thing that we found was that all the services/apps that were for Syrian Refugees were directed towards them, instead of listening from them.
